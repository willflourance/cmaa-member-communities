YoPro Did You Know?

Scott Hamilton will be speaking at the Awards Breakfast at World Conference! Come prepared and read his book, Finish First, before attending!

In his years as a professional ice-skater, Olympic Gold Medalist Scott Hamilton learned to embrace the mind-set of working hard to “beat” the competition. But it seems competition has gotten a bad rap these days. We’ve bought into the belief that it is unfair to participants to rank performance. Yet competition is in fact a good thing because it’s about working toward excellence.

Finish First is a wake-up call for business leaders, entrepreneurs, spouses, parents, and even students to stop settling for mediocre and begin to revitalize their intrinsic will to achieve excellence and go for the win. Most of us feel we were made for something more, but we’re often afraid to allow ourselves to be competitive because we think our finishing first might somehow rob others of their chance to shine. This book encourages the hidden potential, the champion within all of us, to come out—which eventually brings our family, marriage, career, business, and the world around us the greatest possible good.

See Hamilton at the Awards Breakfast on Tuesday, February 26, at 8:00 a.m. Tickets are available at www.cmaa.org/conference.

Carolinas YoPros Play Footgolf in Charlotte

On July 9, following a regional butchery focused education, the Carolinas YoPros took to the golf course to conquer it from a whole new perspective! Twelve YoPros stepped up to the Nine-Hole Footgolf Challenge held at Myers Park Country Club. Each participant had their own soccer ball, and tees were set up in the fairway to shorten the length of the course. The course was undergoing aeration and greens maintenance that week, so instead of cutting large holes in the greens, the club opted to use orange construction fencing and used wooden dowel rods to create a net to secure in the fairway.

The local favorite Olde Mecklenburg Brewery donated several cases of beer to help “hydrate” the players, and the YoPros were off to overcome the course in two groups of six. Scorecards with pars were provided, but the game was played for fun more than anything. The course provided a whole new set of challenges with Footgolf, but it was an overall success for the day.
